## §1. Stop-rule

This is an experience script for Temple Mode — the temporal sequence
of what the operator sees and does inside Temple. It does **not**
implement, build a GUI, render a video, modify the kernel, promote
anything to canon, or commit. It is the behavioral contract Temple
must respect, parallel to the Focus 8-scene loop locked in
`HELEN_OS_V2_INTERACTION_GRAMMAR.md` §2 — but Temple has its own
grammar because Temple is **pre-claim**: drafts do not enter the
spine ledger.

## §3. Why Temple's grammar differs from Focus

Per `HELEN_OS_V2_INTERACTION_GRAMMAR.md` §4, the five-arrow grammar is:

```
click → propose
confirm → mutate
mutate → receipt
receipt → ledger
ledger → memory
```

Temple Mode applies **only the first and last arrows**, with a
modified semantics:

| Arrow | Focus Mode | Temple Mode |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — click → propose | HELEN proposes 3 actions to commit | HELEN proposes 3 *openings* — directions to explore, not actions to execute |
| 2 — confirm → mutate | operator picks one to commit | **does not apply** — Temple has no commitments |
| 3 — mutate → receipt | gate authorizes; runtime executes | **does not apply** — nothing executes outside the operator's own composition |
| 4 — receipt → ledger | spine ledger appends | **does not apply** — drafts go to sanctuary memory, not the spine |
| 5 — ledger → memory | reducer surfaces state | sanctuary memory holds drafts; reducer of the spine never sees them |

This is why Temple says "compose le futur **sans le shipper**." Temple
is the place where the operator can think aloud with HELEN before any
artifact is constitutional. Drafts in Temple have no receipts, no
verdicts, no MAYOR involvement, no ledger, no sovereignty.

## §4. The Temple loop — 8 scenes

Total target duration: ~30s. Parallel structure to Focus, but Temple-
shaped. The example exploration is locked:

> **Canonical example exploration (locked):**
> *"What might HELEN AMP feel like as a sanctuary surface for music
> composition?"*

This is exploratory — no commitment, no ship, no deliverable — and it
demonstrably could not be a Focus Mode intent (Focus intents commit
to work; Temple explorations sit before commitment).

| # | Scene | Duration | Beat |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Sanctuary calm | ~3s | Temple at rest; HELEN composing posture; ambient sacred geometry |
| 2 | Operator opens Temple | ~3s | from the dock module rail; sacred-geometry harmonics deepen subtly |
| 3 | Draft canvas emerges | ~4s | luminous translucent slate, visibly empty / inviting |
| 4 | Operator declares an exploration | ~5s | the canonical example exploration appears |
| 5 | HELEN proposes openings | ~5s | three `[OPENING]` cards (not `[PROPOSAL]`) — directions, not actions |
| 6 | Composition exchange | ~5s | operator + HELEN sketch / annotate; the canvas fills with visibly incomplete shapes |
| 7 | Save to sanctuary draft | ~3s | small confirmation: `Saved to sanctuary · DRAFT · NON_SOVEREIGN · NO_SHIP` |
| 8 | Return to sanctuary calm | ~3s | the canvas dims; the draft persists in sanctuary memory; nothing entered the spine |

Total: ~30s.

## §5. Scene Details

### Scene 1 — Sanctuary calm

- Temple Mode label visible: `TEMPLE MODE`
- HELEN core in **composing posture**: contemplative, slight tilt
  toward an unseen draft surface
- ambient sacred geometry — soft mandala echo deep in background
  (Temple-internal only; never foreground per
  `HELEN_OS_V2_VISUAL_CANON_LOCK.md` §3.4)
- authority pill: `NON_SOVEREIGN · NO_SHIP · DRAFT`
- badge visible: `Symbolic drafting · Non-authoritative`
- no LEGORACLE pill at all (Temple has no active claims by definition)
- no top toggle `FOCUS | WITNESS` — Temple is reached *from* the dock,
  not the toggle (cross-mode rule §2.3.5)

### Scene 2 — Operator opens Temple

- the operator opens Temple from the dock (in terminal: `:temple`;
  in GUI: clicks the Temple module icon)
- a Temple-internal mode rail briefly surfaces:
  `FOCUS · WITNESS · ORACLE · TEMPLE` with **TEMPLE** highlighted
  (informational only — not an actionable cross-mode toggle)
- the sacred-geometry harmonics deepen subtly; the surface darkens
  slightly to indicate transition into sanctuary
- no shipping language anywhere on the surface

### Scene 3 — Draft canvas emerges

- a luminous translucent canvas fades in
- visibly empty / inviting — not a "blank document" but a sanctuary
  slate
- the canvas has no toolbar, no ribbon, no "save" button visible yet
- HELEN's posture turns toward the canvas

### Scene 4 — Operator declares an exploration

- the operator types or speaks the exploration
- the exploration line populates: *"What might HELEN AMP feel like as
  a sanctuary surface for music composition?"*
- this is **not** an intent — there is no commitment implied
- the surface stays still otherwise

### Scene 5 — HELEN proposes openings

- three cards slide in, **labelled `[OPENING]`** (not `[PROPOSAL]`)
- example openings:
  1. `Begin from a single sustained tone — silence as canvas`
  2. `Begin from a memory artifact — a track that already lives here`
  3. `Begin from a constraint — limit yourself to three colors`
- soft glow, ambient sacred-geometry response
- crucially: no policy line, no mutation line, no `[ Confirm ]`
- the operator may or may not pick one; the script does not require it

### Scene 6 — Composition exchange

- the operator picks an opening (or improvises away from all three)
- the canvas fills with visibly incomplete shapes — half-drawn lines,
  half-resolved colors, partial annotations
- HELEN responds in-canvas: a soft annotation, a suggestion of a
  motif, an alternative framing
- this is the **only animated beat** of any duration in Temple — the
  canvas breathes for a moment as composition happens
- nothing is "executed" in the Focus sense; the work is the operator's
  drafting, with HELEN as composing companion

### Scene 7 — Save to sanctuary draft

- small confirmation appears, briefly:
  ```
  Saved to sanctuary
  ─────────────────────────
  authority:  NON_SOVEREIGN
  canon:      NO_SHIP
  lifecycle:  DRAFT
  ```
- **no receipt hash flashes in any spine pill** — because no spine
  event was emitted
- the confirmation fades after ~2 seconds
- sanctuary memory now holds the draft (a sanctuary-internal store —
  not the spine ledger; see §6)

### Scene 8 — Return to sanctuary calm

- the canvas dims, but stays present at low opacity (the draft
  persists in sanctuary memory)
- HELEN returns to the contemplative composing posture of Scene 1
- the authority pill remains `NON_SOVEREIGN · NO_SHIP · DRAFT`
- the operator can either continue exploring (loop back to Scene 4
  with a new exploration) or close Temple and return to Focus

## §6. Sanctuary memory contract

Temple's analog to the spine ledger is **sanctuary memory** — and it
is **explicitly not** the spine ledger.

| Property | Spine ledger (Focus / Witness) | Sanctuary memory (Temple) |
|---|---|---|
| Path | `town/ledger_v1.ndjson` (sovereign) or scope-internal `experiments/helen_mvp_kernel/ledger/events.ndjson` | TBD (proposed: `temple/sanctuary/<session_id>.ndjson`) — never sovereign |
| Hash chain | required (commit-time + replay-time) | optional — drafts may be order-recoverable but not constitutional |
| Author authority | `AI` / `OPERATOR` / `SYSTEM` / `RUNTIME` | `OPERATOR` only — Temple drafts are operator artifacts; HELEN-as-author drafts are still operator-owned |
| Reducer state | spine-level counters, last verdict, terminated flag | sanctuary-internal: draft list, last draft id |
| Visible in Witness Mode | yes | no — Temple drafts are private to the operator; they may inform but never constitute |
| Promotable to canon | through the spine via MAYOR | never — Temple cannot promote to canon without an explicit transition into a Focus / Witness flow that re-authors the artifact constitutionally |

**The transition rule**: a Temple draft may *inspire* a Focus Mode
intent (the operator opens Focus, declares an intent that references
the draft). But the draft itself does not enter the spine. Only the
operator's Focus-Mode commitment, with its own receipt-bearing chain,
enters the spine.

This is the technical expression of "compose le futur sans le shipper."

## §7. Required labels and badges

Every Temple Mode rendering — terminal, GUI, video, image — **must**
display, somewhere visible:

- mode label: `TEMPLE MODE` (active)
- authority pill: `NON_SOVEREIGN · NO_SHIP · DRAFT`
- badge: `Symbolic drafting · Non-authoritative`
- closing-canon footer (at minimum the Temple line):
  > Temple Mode compose le futur sans le shipper.

Forbidden labels (anywhere in Temple):

- the word `SOVEREIGN` (Temple is `NON_SOVEREIGN`)
- `SHIP_AUTHORIZED`, `SHIP_FORBIDDEN`, `READY`, `MERGED`, `DEPLOYED`,
  or any verb implying the artifact has shipped or is ready to ship
- LEGORACLE verdict labels (Temple has no active claims)
- `[PROPOSAL]` (Temple uses `[OPENING]`; reserve `[PROPOSAL]` for Focus
  Mode commitments)
- spine-receipt hashes flashing in pills (no spine events)

---

## §8. Forbidden in Temple Mode experience

Beyond the label rules in §7:

- **No receipt confirmation sheet.** Temple has no commitment; the
  Focus Mode receipt sheet (Scene 5 of the Focus loop) does not appear
- **No execution pulse.** Nothing executes; there is no analog to
  Focus Scene 6
- **No spine ledger flash.** Temple Scene 7 saves to sanctuary, not
  spine
- **No automatic deciding** — even within drafts, HELEN proposes
  openings, never selects
- **No Ralph / W loop** — Temple is contemplative, not iterative-with-
  autonomy
- **No foreground sacred-geometry mandala** (that's Oracle's; Temple's
  geometry is ambient only — see `HELEN_OS_V2_VISUAL_CANON_LOCK.md` §3.4)
- **No leakage into Focus or Witness surfaces.** Temple drafts may
  inform a future Focus intent, but the operator must explicitly
  re-author it there
- **No bright red.** Red is reserved for NO_SHIP / verdict threshold
  in Focus / Witness; Temple is contemplative, not alarmed
